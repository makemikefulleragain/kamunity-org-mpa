Important This template covers common requirements. Always read your specific grant agreement — some funders have different timelines, formats, or evidence requirements. When in doubt, contact your funding officer before submitting.

Part 4: Evidence and Attachments

Required attachments (tick when included)

  • Financial statements / bank reconciliation for grant period
  • Receipts / invoices for major expenditure items
  • Participant attendance records or sign-in sheets
  • Photos or documentation of activities (if required by funder)
  • Survey or feedback results (if required by funder)
  • Signed declaration / authorisation (CEO or Board Chair)

Optional but strengthens acquittal

  • Participant quotes or case studies (de-identified)
  • Media coverage or social media reach data
  • Partnership acknowledgements from collaborating organisations
  • Photos with consent — community in action, not just logos and bunting

Part 5: Declaration

I declare that the information provided in this acquittal report is accurate and complete to the best of my knowledge, and that the grant funds were used for the purposes stated in the grant agreement.

Common Questions — Grant Acquittal

What if I underspent? Contact your funding officer before submitting. Most funders require unspent funds to be returned unless you have written approval to carry over or reallocate. Do not just keep unspent funds.
What if I overspent? Report the actual expenditure. Overspending doesn't automatically mean you get more money — and if you covered the difference yourself, document how. Most funders appreciate transparency over a spotless but inaccurate acquittal.
What if activities changed? Describe what actually happened and why. If you got written approval for the changes, mention it. If you didn't, be honest — explain the circumstances and what you did instead. Funders generally prefer honest variation explanations to silence.
What if I need help? Talk to Kai at kamunity.org — tell it what funder you're acquitting to and what you're stuck on. Or contact Mike Fuller at mike@kamunity.org if you need hands-on help with a complex acquittal.
